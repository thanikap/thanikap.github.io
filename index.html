Featured Research

Machine Learning Prediction of Successful Rocket Launches at NASA Kennedy Using Lightning Data
📍 Cambridge Center for International Research
👤 Dr. Hannah Rana, Mentor
📅 Summer 2025
Publication Pending

Overview: Developed a machine learning model to predict rocket launch success at NASA's Kennedy Space Center by analyzing historical lightning data. This research addresses the significant challenge of weather-related launch delays, which cost millions of dollars and disrupt tightly scheduled missions.

Key Contributions

  • Built predictive model using Kennedy Space Center datasets analyzing windshear, lightning, and rainfall data
  • Integrated Support Vector Machines (SVMs) with SMOTE oversampling to handle imbalanced data (135 successful vs 10 postponed launches)
  • Established 915 V/m electric field threshold for 60% success probability in launch decisions
  • Demonstrated electric field strength as reliable indicator: successful launches never exceeded 839 V/m, postponed launches never fell below 943 V/m

Technical Approach

  • Feature scaling using standardization to normalize electric field measurements
  • SVM hyperplane classification for binary launch/postpone decisions
  • Sigmoid function for probability estimation of launch success
  • SMOTE synthetic data generation to balance training dataset
  • 80/20 train-test split with comprehensive model validation

Impact

  • Created decision support tool reducing reliance on subjective judgment
  • Offers data-driven alternative to existing methods while maintaining safety
  • Demonstrates how environmental sensor data can guide critical decisions through AI
